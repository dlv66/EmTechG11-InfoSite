Q: What is cloud gaming?

A: It is a type of gaming that makes use of high-end game servers that run the game and stream the gameplay to the user’s display.

Q: What are the best cloud gaming services I can use right now?

A: It will depend on your platform of choice. PlayStation? PlayStation Now. Xbox? Xbox Game Pass. PC? GeForce Now or Google Stadia.

Q: What is the number one restriction/problem that keeps cloud gaming from becoming mainstream?

A: The internet. Internet speeds around the world haven’t sped up that much for cloud gaming to be a feasible method of gaming yet. And internet access on its own is a problem especially to those living in 3rd world countries.

Q: Will all games be available to play in any of the 4 main services?

A: No. It will depend on the service you use. Different services have different rights to different games, especially platform-exclusive games. PlayStation Now won’t have access to Xbox nor PC games and vice versa for GeForce Now and Xbox Game Pass.

Q: How is local gaming different from cloud gaming?

A: Local gaming requires you to have the proper hardware in order to play your favorite games. With cloud gaming, your physical hardware doesn’t matter so as long as you have fast internet, a screen, and an input device (such as a controller) because the game company’s servers will be the machines/systems that will run the game then stream the gameplay to your screen via internet.
